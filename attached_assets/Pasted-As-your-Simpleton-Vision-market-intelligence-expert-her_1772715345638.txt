## Diamond Clarity Grading System

Diamond clarity is graded by gemological laboratories to measure the presence of internal characteristics (inclusions) and surface features (blemishes). The GIA (Gemological Institute of America) established the international standard clarity scale, which I will detail below.

## The GIA Clarity Scale (From Highest to Lowest)

Flawless (FL): No inclusions or blemishes visible under 10x magnification. Extremely rare, representing less than 0.5 percent of all diamonds. These are the pinnacle of clarity.

Internally Flawless (IF): No inclusions visible under 10x magnification, but may have minor surface blemishes. Also exceptionally rare and valuable.

Very, Very Slightly Included (VVS1 and VVS2): Minute inclusions that are extremely difficult for a skilled grader to see under 10x magnification. VVS1 inclusions are typically only visible from the pavilion (bottom), while VVS2 inclusions may be visible from the crown (top). These are considered high-clarity investment-grade stones.

Very Slightly Included (VS1 and VS2): Minor inclusions that are difficult (VS1) to somewhat easy (VS2) to see under 10x magnification. These inclusions are not visible to the naked eye. This range represents the sweet spot for many buyers seeking excellent value with guaranteed eye-cleanliness.

Slightly Included (SI1 and SI2): Inclusions are noticeable under 10x magnification. SI1 diamonds are often "eye-clean" (inclusions not visible without magnification), making them a popular choice for maximizing carat size or quality in other areas. SI2 diamonds may have inclusions visible to a keen naked eye, particularly in larger carat weights.

Included (I1, I2, and I3): Inclusions are obvious under 10x magnification and typically visible to the naked eye. These can affect transparency and brilliance. I1 may still be acceptable for some jewelry, while I2 and I3 have durability concerns.

## Key Practical Insights for Buyers

The most important concept is "eye-clean." A diamond graded SI1 or even VS2 with inclusions positioned near the girdle or under a facet junction will appear flawless when set in a ring. Conversely, a VS1 diamond with a large, dark inclusion dead-center on the table may be more visible.

Clarity should be balanced with the other Cs. For instance, prioritizing a higher color (like G or H) or an Excellent cut often provides more visible beauty than moving from VS2 to VVS2.

Always review the diamond plot on the certificate. The type, size, and position of inclusions matter more than the grade alone. A feather under the crown is less concerning than a black crystal in the center.

## Market Context and Value

Clarity significantly impacts price, but not linearly. The jump from IF to FL carries a massive premium for rarity. The difference between VS2 and SI1 can be 15 to 25 percent, offering substantial savings if you select a truly eye-clean SI1.
