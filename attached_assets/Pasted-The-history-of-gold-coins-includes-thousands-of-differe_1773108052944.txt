The history of gold coins includes thousands of different types, denominations, and designs spanning from the first Lydian coins in the 7th century BC to modern bullion. While it is impossible to list every historical type, major gold coin types are generally categorized by era (ancient, medieval, and modern) and by issuing nation, with most circulating gold coins ceasing production by the 1930s in favor of modern bullion coins.

Characteristics of Gold Coin Types
Weight & Purity: Pre-1933 coins are typically 90% (21.6k) gold, while modern bullion is often 99.9% or 99.99% (24k) gold.
Denominations: Early coins often traded by weight, while later coins (19th century) established face values (e.g., $10 Eagle, 20 Francs).
Typesets: Collectors often build "type sets," aiming to acquire one of each distinct design rather than every date.
